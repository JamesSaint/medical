Reported Symptoms & Clinical Course

The following symptoms were documented across referrals and follow-up consultations prior to and following colonoscopy. They reflect both initial presentation and ongoing functional effects.

  • Abdominal Pain: Persistent left-sided discomfort radiating toward the groin, described as a deep “stitch-like” pain that can intensify after meals or prolonged sitting.
  • Bowel Urgency: Morning urgency to open bowels, typically 3–4 episodes over a few hours with an ongoing sensation of incomplete evacuation.
  • Rectal Bleeding: Two-week episode of bright red blood in stool in the prior year, not recurrent since.
  • Bloating & Distension: Noted particularly in the evenings, accompanied by sensations of fullness or trapped gas.
  • Appetite & Weight Change: Decreased appetite and unintentional weight loss of approximately 8–10 kg over 18 months, initially associated with dietary changes.
  • General Function: Increased bowel frequency and left-sided tenderness correlated with muscular hypertrophy of the sigmoid colon, likely contributing to cramping and urgency.

Summary: The pattern of symptoms is consistent with functional disturbance secondary to sigmoid diverticulosis rather than active inflammatory bowel disease. No ongoing bleeding or systemic inflammatory features were observed.

Sources: Referral (24 Mar 2025, Dr M Mottaleb), Initial Consultation (10 Apr 2025, Dr Ellis), and Post-Procedure Letters (May–Aug 2025).

Key Findings

  • Moderate sigmoid diverticulosis without significant inflammatory change. Biopsies reported as normal.
  • Single colonic polyp removed. No surveillance colonoscopy recommended based on this finding alone.
  • One or two aphthous ulcers in the terminal ileum, judged to be of doubtful significance and likely physiological.
  • Segmental muscular hypertrophy noted in the sigmoid colon, which could relate to left sided abdominal discomfort and more frequent bowel action.

Summarised from the post colonoscopy outcome letter dated 16 August 2025.
