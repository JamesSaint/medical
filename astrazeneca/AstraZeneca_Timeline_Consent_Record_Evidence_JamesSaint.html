At a Glance

  • Development began Jan–Apr 2020 by Oxford Jenner Institute; partnership with AstraZeneca announced 30 Apr 2020.
  • UK authorisation granted 30 Dec 2020.
  • EMA confirmed very rare TTS risk on 7 Apr 2021.
  • JCVI advised alternatives for 30–39 when available on 7 May 2021.
  • EU marketing authorisation withdrawn 27 Mar 2024 (effective 7 May 2024).
  • Global discontinuation reported May 2024 AP, Guardian.

Informed-Consent Context — 1977 Birth Year

  • 7 Apr 2021: EMA lists thrombosis with thrombocytopenia as a very rare side effect and updates product information EMA PRAC.
  • 7 May 2021: JCVI advises alternatives for adults 30–39 without risk factors where available JCVI Statement.
  • Recipient information: patient leaflets and UK recipient pages maintained on Gov.uk PIL.
For a 43–44 year-old, formal TTS warnings were issued from 7 Apr 2021. After that date, disclosure should have been part of updated materials. JCVI did not restrict AstraZeneca for those over 40.

Patient Statement & Discrepancy Analysis

Patient Statement (James Saint): “Both COVID-19 vaccine doses were administered at my home by two nurses from Emsworth Medical Practice. I was house-bound with severe PTSD and agoraphobia. I did not feel I was given informed consent or appropriate care for a house-bound patient.”

Documented Records:

  • Fig. 1 (31-Mar-2021 SMS) indicates a planned home visit for vaccination on 1 April.
  • Fig. 2 (01-Apr-2021 entry) records first-dose details but no location in the extract.
  • Fig. 3 (22-Jun-2021 entry) records second dose with site code N9J0U — Emsworth Baptist Church, conflicting with the home-administration account.
  • Fig. 4 (21-Dec-2021 note) documents patient reporting a severe reaction to the second dose with ongoing nerve pain and holding off booster.
  • Fig. 5 (07-Sep-2022) and Fig. 6 (07-Jun-2024) show continuing clinical references linking symptoms/concerns to COVID vaccination and a breakdown of trust.

Analysis: The record set shows (a) a location discrepancy across doses, and (b) subsequent notes of significant adverse experience and ongoing attribution to the COVID-19 vaccine. Audit logs (NIVS/Pinnacle), site-code mapping, and a consent review should be undertaken; if appropriate, entries should be corrected or annotated with this statement.

Record Accuracy & Rectification

  • Request NIVS/Pinnacle event logs for both doses, including site code used and vaccinator IDs.
  • Request written confirmation of the administering address for each dose, or an explanation for site-code attribution when a home visit was performed.
