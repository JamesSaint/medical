At a Glance

  • Development began Jan–Apr 2020 by Oxford Jenner Institute; partnership with AstraZeneca announced 30 Apr 2020.
  • UK authorisation granted 30 Dec 2020.
  • EMA confirmed very rare TTS risk on 7 Apr 2021.
  • JCVI advised alternatives for 30–39 when available on 7 May 2021.
  • EU marketing authorisation withdrawn 27 Mar 2024 (effective 7 May 2024).
  • Global discontinuation reported May 2024 AP, Guardian.

Patient Statement & Discrepancy Analysis

Patient Statement (James Saint): “Both COVID-19 vaccine doses were administered at my home by two nurses from Emsworth Medical Practice. I was house-bound at the time with severe PTSD and agoraphobia. I did not feel I was given informed consent or appropriate care for a house-bound patient. The visit felt rushed and impersonal.”

Documented Records:

  • Fig. 1 (31-Mar-2021 SMS) indicates a planned home visit for vaccination on 1 April.
  • Fig. 2 (01-Apr-2021 entry) records first-dose administration details (product, batch, site=left deltoid) but no location is specified in the extract.
  • Fig. 3 (22-Jun-2021 entry) records second dose with site code N9J0U — Emsworth Baptist Church, which conflicts with the patient’s account of a home administration.

Analysis: The record set contains an internal inconsistency regarding administration location across the two doses. The contemporaneous SMS supports home attendance on 1 April; the second-dose entry lists a mass-site code. This discrepancy should be addressed via audit logs (e.g., NIVS/Pinnacle event data, site-code mapping) and, if appropriate, corrected or annotated with a patient statement.

Note: The statements above reflect the patient’s account. Determinations about consent adequacy and documentation accuracy should be made by reviewing clinicians and/or the practice after examining the complete record and audit trail.
