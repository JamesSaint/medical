Symptom Overview

  • Throat & Facial Tension: Left-sided throat and facial tightness, intermittent jaw and ear pressure, voice fatigue, and a persistent phlegm sensation (possible vagus nerve involvement).
    • Functional impact: Voice tires with extended conversation or reading aloud; meals and hydration are slower on high-symptom days; public speaking or long calls increase strain and recovery time.
  • Leg, Foot & Groin: Left calf heaviness, foot instability on uneven ground, intermittent tingling or numbness, groin and testicular pain, and pelvic floor tension (possible L4–S1 and pudendal/genitofemoral nerve involvement).
    • Functional impact: Walking distance and pace reduced during flares; balance and single-leg stance feel less secure on the left; sitting tolerance affected by pelvic discomfort; sexual comfort and function fluctuate.
  • Sensory & Pain: Occipital area pain and pressure, intermittent facial and scalp paresthesia (especially on the left), and occasional light-touch or temperature asymmetry.
    • Functional impact: Sustained screen time, bright lighting, or head movements increase occipital pain; sleep can be disrupted, reducing next-day stamina.
  • Visual & Vestibular: Intermittent disturbance in the left visual field, eye strain, and dizziness with rapid head turns.
    • Functional impact: Reading small text and multitasking are slower; visually busy environments increase fatigue; prefers larger fonts, reduced motion, and paced gaze changes.
  • Cognitive & Energy: Mental fatigue, word-finding lapses, slower processing under cognitive load, and reduced stress tolerance.
    • Functional impact: Complex tasks benefit from chunking and written checklists; requires recovery time after demanding activity; decision quality drops when multitasking or overtired.
  • Autonomic & General: Occasional “heart-in-throat” sensations, stress reactivity, and temperature intolerance during flares.
    • Functional impact: Requires calm start-up for demanding interactions; benefits from breathing resets, gentle pacing, and consistent daily rhythm.
