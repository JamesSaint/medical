Clinical Summary – Carbamazepine Intolerance

Prepared: 12 Oct 2025

Context

History of intolerance to duloxetine, gabapentin, pregabalin, and amitriptyline with autonomic and histaminic features. Carbamazepine 100 mg was trialled as a reluctant final conventional option under neurology guidance.

Recent Events

11 Oct 2025 Autonomic episode after a warm bath at ~13:00, approximately 22 hours after 100 mg carbamazepine (15:00 on 10 Oct). Dizziness, near-syncope, pulse variability 111 → 85 → 110 bpm, chest and left arm tightness, facial tingling, occipital constriction, headache. Sat on the floor until stable. Considered calling 111/ambulance. Full entry.
12 Oct 2025 Generalised pruritus several hours after second 100 mg dose: scalp, underarms, chest, groin; heat–cold mismatch; sleep disrupted; no visible rash at time of logging. Full entry.
12 Oct 2025 111 called. Ambulance attendance after ~2.5 hours. Took chlorphenamine 4mg, cooled outside, nicotine used, symptoms improved prior to arrival. Paramedics recorded stable vitals and normal ECG; discharged with advice to follow up GP/neurology.

Clinical Interpretation

Pattern is consistent with autonomic and mast-cell activation in response to neuro-active agents, now including carbamazepine. This mirrors prior reactions to duloxetine and tricyclics where antihistamines were advised with only marginal help. The reactions are physiological and reproducible across classes.

Requests to Care Team

  • Record carbamazepine as intolerant / adverse reaction.
  • Consider mast-cell workup (baseline and event tryptase, plasma/urine histamine, DAO).
  • Consider pharmacogenomic profiling for CYP2D6/CYP3A4 and related pathways before new prescriptions.
  • Discuss Nabiximols (THC/CBD oromucosal spray) as preferred therapeutic route given intolerance to sodium-channel and serotonergic agents.
  • Provide guidance on safe discontinuation and washout period following last dose.

Patient Statement

I have been reluctant with duloxetine, pregabalin, gabapentin, and amitriptyline due to prior experiences. Carbamazepine has produced similar adverse patterns without pain relief. I wish to stop it now and focus on a treatment pathway that does not trigger autonomic or mast-cell reactions. I request support in accessing Nabiximols through NHS pathways or exceptional-case review.
