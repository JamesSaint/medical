Consideration of Nabiximols (Sativex®) Trial for Central Neuropathic Pain

Subject: Consideration of Nabiximols (Sativex®) Trial for Central Neuropathic Pain
Date: 8 October 2025

This correspondence forms part of the ongoing multidisciplinary review of my neurological case led by Dr. Omar Malik at Mount Alvernia Hospital, Guildford

Dear Dr Malik,

Following our recent discussions regarding the likely central origin of my neuropathic symptoms, and in light of my ongoing left-sided pain and sensory disturbance, I have been exploring evidence-based options for patients who do not tolerate, or gain limited benefit from, standard first-line agents.

Given my poor tolerance of antidepressant and anticonvulsant medication, I would like to ask whether a monitored trial of Nabiximols (Sativex®) might be clinically appropriate. The balanced THC–CBD formulation appears relevant to central post-stroke or brainstem-related neuropathic pain, acting on both nociceptive modulation and neuroinflammatory pathways.

While its UK licence currently covers multiple-sclerosis-related spasticity, published evidence increasingly supports cautious off-label use for other central neuropathic pain syndromes. I would value your guidance on whether this could be considered within my current management plan, particularly as part of the ongoing multidisciplinary review.

If you felt this justified, I would greatly appreciate your advice on:

  • Whether a short monitored trial could be arranged under your supervision or via a pain specialist in your network
  • Any formal criteria, consent, or funding routes required (including AXA support)
  • Appropriate starting and titration parameters

I fully appreciate the need for evidence-based prescribing and would approach this cautiously and collaboratively. My hope is to find an option that could improve quality of life and functional stability without recourse to higher-risk pharmacological agents.

Thank you again for your care and ongoing consideration of my case.

Warm regards,
James Saint
