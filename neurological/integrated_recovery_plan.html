Integrated Recovery Plan

Purpose

This integrated plan coordinates supplements, non-drug neuromodulation, and an adjunct cannabinoid option (THC:CBD oromucosal) to reduce central neuropathic pain, improve sleep and autonomic stability, and support neuroregeneration following a right-sided brainstem stroke.

Chosen for best clinical fit, evidence, and cost–benefit. Start at the top and layer gradually.

Adjunct Pharmacological Option — THC:CBD (Sativex®/Nabiximols)

Objective: Non‑inhaled, balanced cannabinoid therapy to lower central pain signalling and improve sleep when first‑line neuropathic agents are not tolerated.

  • Form: Oromucosal spray (preferred) or oral oil — avoids inhalation (important with emphysema).
  • Mechanism: THC reduces central hyperexcitability; CBD calms glial activation and mitigates THC side‑effects; 1:1 ratio provides balanced analgesia.
  • Trial approach: Start 1 spray at night; titrate by +1 spray every 2–3 days to effect (typical 4–8 sprays/day). Reassess at 2–3 weeks.
  • Monitor: Pain floor, sleep quality, mood, orthostatic symptoms, and BP weekly.
  • Status: Enquiries sent to Lyphe, Curaleaf, Releaf, and Zerenia. Sapphire is a fallback if access to Sativex is limited.

Use only under specialist supervision; coordinate with neurology/pain teams. Avoid inhaled/vaped products.

Daily Rhythm (steady state)

  • Morning: Aug NAC 600 mg; ALCAR 500–1000 mg; Breathwork 10 min; tVNS 15–30 min; optional MCT with breakfast.
  • Lunch: R‑ALA 300 mg with food; Omega‑3; Curcumin (when added).
  • Mid‑afternoon: Aug NAC 600 mg; PEA (if used).
  • Evening: Omega‑3; Curcumin; short Mirror Therapy set.
  • Bedtime: Magnesium 200–400 mg; 5–10 min breathing practice.
