Autonomic Episode After Bath

Date: 11 Oct 2025

Entry Summary

Bath time13:00
Carbamazepine dose100 mg at 15:00 on 10 Oct 2025 (about 22 hours prior)
ContextWarm bath during early carbamazepine use.
Primary symptomsDizziness, near-faint, chest tightness, left arm tightness, facial tingling, occipital tightness, headache.
PulseFluctuated ~111 bpm down to ~85 bpm, then back to ~110 bpm.
Oxygen95% at lowest reported, recovered to 99% after cooling.
ConsciousnessNo loss of consciousness. Sat on the floor until stable.
ResolutionImproved with cooling, rest, and leaving the bath.

Timeline

Approx onset13:00 while in bath
Peak symptomsWithin minutes. Needed to exit bath and sit on floor.
Approx resolutionImproved over ~30-45 minutes after cooling and resting.

Symptoms and Physical Sensations

  • Sudden dizziness and feeling you might collapse.
  • Rapid pulse changes 111 → 85 → 110 bpm recorded by finger oximeter.
  • Chest pain and tightness with left arm tightness.
  • Tingling in face and a constricted feeling at the back of head and along spine.
  • Headache.
  • Intense distress and fear of needing emergency services.

Distress and Communication Attempts

  • Called Cliff, no answer.
  • Called Jo, no answer initially. Jo returned the call and stayed on the phone during the episode while symptoms fluctuated.
  • Considered calling 111 or ambulance due to severity.

Interpretation

Event most consistent with heat-triggered autonomic reactivity during early carbamazepine use with residual plasma levels present at ~22 hours post dose. Differential includes vasovagal response and other autonomic instability. Cardiac or neurovascular causes cannot be excluded based on symptoms alone and would require assessment if recurrent.

Self Care Actions

  • Exited bath, cooled environment, light layers.
  • Hydration and seated rest until stable.
  • Avoid hot baths or saunas during titration.

Red Flags

  • If chest pain becomes severe or persistent, or shortness of breath develops, call 999.
  • If fainting occurs or neurological deficits appear, call 999.
  • For recurrence of similar episode, seek urgent clinical advice.

Notes for Clinician

  • Please advise on dose timing, titration, and suitability versus alternatives given autonomic sensitivity.
