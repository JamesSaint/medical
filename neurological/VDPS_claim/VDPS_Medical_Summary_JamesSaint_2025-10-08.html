VDPS Medical Summary

Prepared for the UK Vaccine Damage Payment Scheme. Updated 2025-10-08.

Declaration

I, James Saint, submit this medical summary in support of my Vaccine Damage Payment Scheme claim. On the balance of probabilities, my neurological injury was caused by the AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine administered in 2021.

Clinical and Diagnostic Chronology

  • 24 April 2025 — Private referral via Doctor Care Anywhere for left occipital to eyebrow burning pain, left jaw and collarbone sensory disturbance, left ear pressure, visual disturbance, and localized hair loss along the pain path. Temporal onset recorded within 24 hours of the AstraZeneca vaccine in 2021.
  • 15 May 2025 — Consultant Neurologist assessment noted brisk reflexes, definite left-sided vibration loss over pelvis, possible left pupil asymmetry with pale disc. Working impression of a high cervical lesion and cranial neuropathy.
  • 10 July 2025 — MRI reported a right cerebellar infarct of uncertain cause and subtle C5/6 signal change possibly consistent with myelitis. Vascular work-up and CSF planned.
  • 31 July 2025 — Lumbar puncture performed. CSF sent for cell count, protein, glucose, oligoclonal bands, ACE, and Borrelia.
  • 14 August 2025 — Radiology review confirmed the right cerebellar infarct. CT angiogram and bubble echocardiogram were normal. CSF was normal with negative oligoclonal bands and Borrelia. Consultant letter states that it is highly likely that I had a post AstraZeneca vaccine related myelitis.
  • 7 October 2025 — 3 Tesla MRI of the cervical spine showed no cord signal change. Given persistent left hemisensory neuropathic pain and the presence of the right cerebellar infarct, my neurologist suspects a small brainstem stroke in the same posterior circulation territory and states that the thromboembolic event is more likely related to the vaccine.

Investigations

  • MRI brain — confirmed right cerebellar infarct.
  • MRI cervical spine — initial subtle signal at C5/6 on 1.5 Tesla study, no signal change on 3 Tesla review.
  • CT angiogram — intra and extracranial vessels without structural cause.
  • Bubble echocardiogram — no paradoxical embolus identified.
  • CSF — normal cell count, protein, and glucose. Oligoclonal bands negative. ACE and Borrelia negative.

Current Condition and Impact

I continue to experience persistent left-sided hemisensory neuropathic pain with occipital involvement. These symptoms have a material impact on my function and quality of life.

Causation Statement

Considering the timing of symptom onset after vaccination, the presence of a posterior circulation cerebellar infarct, the later suspicion of a small brainstem stroke in the same territory, and the exclusion of alternative vascular and autoimmune causes, the evidence supports vaccine causation on the balance of probabilities. This meets the VDPS threshold.
