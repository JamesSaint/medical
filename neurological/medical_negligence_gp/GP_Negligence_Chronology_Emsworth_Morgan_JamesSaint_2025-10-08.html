GP Negligence Chronology

Emsworth Medical, Dr Hannah Morgan. Prepared for preliminary legal review. Updated 2025-10-08.

Background

From 2021 through 2024 I was registered at Emsworth Medical, Emsworth Surgery, under Dr Hannah Morgan. Within 24 hours of my AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine in 2021 I developed left-sided occipital burning pain, facial sensory disturbance, ear pressure, and visual symptoms. These symptoms recurred episodically and later persisted. Despite repeated presentations there is no record of timely neurology referral or cross-sectional neuroimaging being initiated.

Chronology of Missed Escalations

Period Presentation Standard of Care Expectation Observed Management
2021 Acute unilateral occipital pain, facial numbness, visual disturbance within 24 hours of vaccination. Prompt neurological assessment and consideration of imaging, documentation of vaccine timing, and MHRA Yellow Card reporting. Reassurance and symptomatic advice noted. No urgent referral documented.
2022 Recurrent episodes of focal sensory symptoms. Neurology referral and MRI brain and cervical spine given ongoing focal neurological features. No escalation recorded.
2023 Persistent symptoms with functional impact. Escalation to secondary care with documented differential including post-vaccine neurological injury. No referral recorded.
2024 Continuing focal symptoms. Referral and imaging as above. No referral recorded.
Apr 2025 Private referral via Doctor Care Anywhere due to lack of progress in primary care. — Neurology and full work-up commenced privately, revealing a right cerebellar infarct and subsequent stroke-focused pathway.

Corroborating Specialist Evidence

  • Right cerebellar infarct confirmed on MRI brain. No structural vascular cause on CT angiogram or bubble echocardiogram.
  • CSF normal with no oligoclonal bands or inflammatory markers.
  • Revised consultant opinion favors a small brainstem stroke in the same posterior circulation territory with vaccine-related thromboembolic mechanism considered more likely.

Breach and Harm

  • Failure to escalate focal neurological symptoms to urgent specialist review and imaging between 2021 and 2024.
  • Failure to document and act upon the temporal association with vaccination.
  • Prolonged diagnostic delay with persistent hemisensory neuropathic pain and functional impact.
