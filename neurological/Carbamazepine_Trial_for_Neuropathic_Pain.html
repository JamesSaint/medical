Carbamazepine Trial for Neuropathic Pain Summary

Date Begun: 10 October 2025

What it is

Carbamazepine is an established anticonvulsant used for epilepsy and nerve pain. It stabilises over active nerve firing by blocking voltage gated sodium channels. That quiets burning, shooting, or electric sensations that come from irritated nerves.

Why it fits here

  • Aim is to reduce left sided neuropathic pain likely from central or brainstem pathway irritation.
  • Older agent with predictable behaviour and clear monitoring.
  • Alternative options like duloxetine, pregablin, gabapentin, tramadol and amitriptyline were not tolerated.

How to start

  • Typical initial dose: 100 to 200 mg once daily with food. Many start at night.
  • Titration: increase every 3 to 7 days toward 200 mg twice daily, then adjust to effect. Common range 400 to 800 mg per day.
  • Form: modified release often best for steady levels and fewer peaks.
  • Do not stop suddenly. Any change should be a gradual taper with clinician advice.

Monitoring

  • Blood tests after a few weeks: carbamazepine level, liver enzymes, sodium, full blood count.
  • Therapeutic level typically 4 to 12 µg/mL. Clinical response and tolerability are the priority.
  • Bring a symptom and side effect log to review appointments.

Common effects

  • Drowsiness, dizziness, mild nausea, blurred vision, unsteady feeling during dose changes.
  • Usually ease within 1 to 2 weeks at a stable dose.
  • Take with food. Rise slowly from sitting. Avoid driving if groggy.

Red flags — seek medical advice

  • New rash, mouth sores, facial swelling, fever.
  • Confusion, severe headache, worsening unsteadiness, nausea that does not settle.
  • Yellowing of eyes, dark urine, sore throat that persists, easy bruising.
  • Palpitations, fainting spells.

Interactions

  • Carbamazepine speeds up liver metabolism of many medicines. Always tell prescribers and pharmacists.
  • Alcohol increases drowsiness and liver load.
  • Avoid grapefruit products unless cleared by your clinician.

Quick start plan

  1. Start at the agreed low dose with your evening meal.
  2. Record daily pain level, function, and any side effects.
  3. Increase only as instructed. Pause titration if side effects bite.
  4. Arrange bloods at 2 to 4 weeks or as directed.
  5. Review with Dr Malik, bringing your log.

Questions for your clinician

  • Target dose for my case and when to hold or reduce.
  • When to do levels and labs next.
  • Backup options if pain relief is partial or side effects persist.
