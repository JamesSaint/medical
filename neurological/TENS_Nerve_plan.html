TENS Nerve Rehabilitation Plan

Tailored to left‑sided symptoms. Device: Beurer EM49.

1. Occipital and Upper Cervical

Targets C2–C4 and occipital nerves for scalp burning and temple ache relief.

  • Channel A: One pad just below skull base behind the left ear; second pad 3–4 cm inferior along the paraspinal line.
  • Program: TENS pain mode (80–100 Hz), 20 minutes.

2. Shoulder and Upper Limb Tension

Targets trapezius and posterior deltoid to ease brachial plexus irritation and arm paresthesia.

  • Channel A or B: One pad mid‑trapezius (left), second over posterior deltoid.
  • Program: TENS pain mode or gentle EMS recovery, 10–20 minutes.

3. Thoracic / Flank Tightness

Targets intercostal and dorsal cutaneous nerves for left thoracic tightness.

  • Channel A or B: Two pads horizontally along the left mid‑back at symptom height, ~5 cm apart.
  • Program: TENS pain mode, 80 Hz, 20 minutes.

4. Lower Abdomen and Groin Line

Indirect access to ilioinguinal / genitofemoral pathways. Avoid genital area.

  • Channel A or B: One pad just inside the left iliac crest; second on upper inner thigh.
  • Program: TENS low intensity (40–60 Hz), 15 minutes.

5. Lumbar / Hip Flexor Line

Targets paraspinals and anterior thigh for psoas / hip flexor tightness.

  • Channel A: One pad lateral lumbar paraspinals (L3–L4); second on upper anterior thigh.
  • Program: Gentle EMS recovery, ~10 minutes.

6. Calf and Foot Line

Targets sciatic / tibial pathway for calf tingling and proprioception.

  • Channel B: One pad mid‑hamstring; second mid‑calf or soleus.
  • Program: TENS pain mode, 80 Hz, 20 minutes.

Quick Use Guide

  • Begin at intensity 3–4. Increase only if comfortable.
  • Session length 20–30 minutes; 1–2 sessions daily per area.
  • Rotate areas across the week to avoid skin fatigue.
  • Log settings and effects in your recovery tracker to refine placements.
Gold blocks indicate pad positions. Adjust a few centimetres to find the sweet spot.
