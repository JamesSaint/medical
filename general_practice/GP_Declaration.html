Personal Medical Statement

To my General Practitioner,

I want to be transparent about how I engage with healthcare. My confidence in the current GP model is limited. Too often it defaults to prescription and protocol, with little space for critical thinking, curiosity, or accountability.

I’m not here to challenge you personally, but to engage with clarity and mutual respect. I approach my health from an evidence-based yet holistic standpoint and take full ownership of the decisions concerning my body. I ask that you meet me as an informed adult seeking collaboration, not compliance.

I value practitioners who think independently, question assumptions, and recognise the physiological and environmental complexity behind symptoms rather than reducing everything to medication management.

My position isn’t theoretical. I have both first-hand and second-hand experience of the harms caused by overprescription, misdiagnosis, and the absence of professional accountability within this system. Those experiences have shaped how I now approach healthcare, with vigilance, evidence, and self-responsibility.

You are a public servant, entrusted with acting in the best interests of those you serve. I expect that duty to be honoured. I will not engage in performative compliance or tolerate ego, assumption, or unexamined protocol. I am here to collaborate intelligently in pursuit of truth and recovery, nothing less.

Healthcare is evolving rapidly. With the speed of AI and data-driven diagnostics, the traditional GP model is already becoming obsolete. Within a few short years, patients will access more objective, personalised, and accountable insight than this system currently provides. I mention this not to be misiterpreted as a threat, but as a reminder that humility, adaptability, and genuine curiosity will soon matter more than credentials.

If we can meet in that spirit, we’ll work effectively together. If not, I prefer to disengage early rather than waste either of our time.

Sincerely,
James Saint
